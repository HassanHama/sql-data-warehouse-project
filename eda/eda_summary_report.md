# Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Report: Retail Data Warehouse Audit

## 📌 Project Overview
This report summarizes the findings from a comprehensive Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) performed on the **Gold Layer** of the Medallion Architecture Data Warehouse. The goal was to validate data integrity, identify performance trends, and provide actionable business insights for the retail dataset.

---

## 🛠️ Technical Implementation
- **Environment:** MySQL
- **Architecture:** Medallion (Bronze → Silver → Gold)
- **Key Techniques:** Data Profiling, Window Functions, Temporal Analysis, Referential Integrity Audits.

---

## 🔍 Data Quality & Integrity Audit
The audit revealed several critical findings that impact reporting accuracy:

*   **Surrogate Key Duplication (SCD Type 2):** Identified 6 natural keys (`customer_id`) mapped to multiple surrogate keys. Specifically, `customer_id` **29466** exists as three separate records due to daily snapshot ingestion without de-duplication.
*   **The "ID 0" Anomaly:** Discovered four unique customer keys tied to `customer_id = 0`, representing approximately **$4,992** in revenue from unidentified or guest accounts.
*   **Referential Integrity:** The audit confirmed that while there is a slight discrepancy in distinct counts (18,490 vs 18,485), all `customer_key` values in the Fact table have corresponding entries in the Dimension table, ruling out orphaned transactions.

## 📈 Business Insights

### 1. Product Performance
*   **Revenue Concentration:** The **Bikes** category is the primary revenue driver, accounting for **96%** ($28.3M) of total lifetime sales.
*   **Top Product:** The **Mountain-200 Black- 46** is the highest-grossing individual item ($1,373,454).
*   **Inactive Inventory:** The **Components** category contains 127 products but recorded **$0 in sales**, suggesting these may be internal parts or have missing transaction logs.

### 2. Geographic & Demographic Analysis
*   **Regional Efficiency:** While the **United States** has the largest customer base (7,482), **Australia** shows significantly higher value per customer. Australia generates nearly equal revenue (~$9.06M) with less than half the customer count (3,591).
*   **Gender Balance:** The customer base is nearly evenly split between Male (9,344) and Female (9,129).

### 3. Temporal Trends & Seasonality
*   **Peak Season:** Counter-intuitively for the cycling industry, **Autumn** is the peak sales season ($8.4M), followed closely by Summer.
*   **Growth Curve:** The business experienced massive scaling in **2013**, which accounts for over 55% of the total recorded revenue.

## 💡 Recommendations
1.  **Deduplication Logic:** Implement UPSERT logic in the Silver-to-Gold ETL pipeline to handle Slowly Changing Dimensions (SCD) and prevent duplicate customer counts.
2.  **Targeted Marketing:** Increase marketing spend in **Australia** to capitalize on high Average Order Value (AOV).
3.  **Inventory Review:** Investigate the "Components" category to determine if products are being under-reported or if they should be retired from the Gold Layer view.
